## Anticipated Interview Questions & Answers

### Technical Questions

**Q: What experience do you have with concrete quantity takeoffs?**
> *"At King Properties, I handled concrete takeoffs for high-rise commercial and residential projects—foundations, columns, slabs, shear walls. I used Planswift to measure volumes and rebar quantities. I also reviewed structural drawings to ensure accuracy and flagged discrepancies early. Recently, as an independent consultant, I've continued doing concrete takeoffs for residential projects, but I'm very comfortable scaling to commercial scopes."*

**Q: Are you familiar with On-Screen Takeoff?**
> *"I've used Planswift extensively, which is similar in functionality. I've started learning OST through tutorials because I saw it's important for this role. I'm a quick learner—when King Properties introduced new software, I was always the first on my team to master it."*

**Q: How do you handle addenda and RFIs during bidding?**
> *"I always download the addenda immediately and review them against my takeoffs. I maintain a revision log in Excel to track changes. If an RFI affects quantities, I flag it to the estimator team and adjust my takeoffs accordingly. I never assume—I always verify."*

**Q: How do you ensure accuracy in your takeoffs?**
> *"I use a double-check system. First, I do a full takeoff. Then I review against the scope sheet and specifications. For concrete, I cross-check volume calculations against rebar quantities—if something looks off, I revisit the drawings. I also maintain organized files so anyone on the team can review my work."*

**Q: Have you worked with U.S. construction drawings before?**
> *"Yes, I've worked on Australian residential projects, which follow similar standards to the U.S. I'm comfortable with imperial measurements, architectural and structural sheets, and typical U.S. notation. I can adapt quickly."*

### Behavioral Questions

**Q: Describe a time you had to work under a tight bid deadline.**
> *(STAR)*
> - **Situation:** At King Properties, we had a 3-day turnaround for a Php 100M+ project bid.
> - **Task:** I needed to complete all concrete and structural takeoffs accurately.
> - **Action:** I prioritized critical scope, used Planswift efficiently, and communicated daily with the estimator. I worked extra hours but maintained accuracy.
> - **Result:** We submitted on time and won the project.

**Q: How do you handle feedback or corrections on your takeoffs?**
> *"I welcome feedback—it makes me better. If someone catches an error, I note it, correct it immediately, and review my process to prevent recurrence. At King Properties, I created a checklist based on common feedback to improve my team's accuracy."*

**Q: Why do you want to work for a U.S.-based company?**
> *"I enjoy the challenge of working with international standards and larger-scale commercial projects. This role allows me to apply my QS expertise while learning U.S. construction practices. I'm also drawn to the stability and growth potential of working with a specialized concrete contractor."*

### Logistics Questions

**Q: Are you comfortable working Pacific Time hours?**
> *"Yes, I've adjusted my schedule before for Australian clients. I'm flexible and can arrange my day to align with PST. I'll ensure I'm available during core hours and maintain reliable internet."*

**Q: Do you have a quiet workspace and reliable internet?**
> *"Yes, I have a dedicated home office with fiber internet, backup power, and a quiet environment. I also have two monitors set up for efficient takeoff work."*

- "What does a typical project timeline look like from bid to completion?"
